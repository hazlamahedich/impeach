The demand-risk premise in your brief is **outdated**. The impeachment is not hypothetical — it is happening now:

- **Second impeachment of Sara Duterte** is ACTIVE. House voted **257–25** to impeach on May 11, 2026; Senate convened as impeachment court May 18, 2026; **trial expected to begin July 6, 2026** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_impeachment_of_Sara_Duterte)).

**Implication:** IIP has a live 6–12 month demand window starting July 2026. This compresses and intensifies the launch sequencing — the soft launch MUST coincide with the trial.

## 3. SAM Analysis — IIP v1 (Impeachment-Focused)

The SAM is **event-driven and narrower than TAM**. Demand is binary: active impeachment = high; dormant = low.

### Who realistically uses an impeachment-specific platform?

| Segment | Pool | Realistic Reach | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Journalists covering impeachment | 50–200 active | ~150 (core) + 500 (peripheral) | Beat reporters at PCIJ, VERA Files, Rappler, Inquirer, GMA, ABS-CBN, plus provincial press |
| Legal professionals (interested subset) | 78,785 IBP | 5,000–15,000 | Constitutional law specialists, bar reviewers, law faculty, litigation support |
| Academics (pol sci, law, journalism) | ~10,000 | 2,000–5,000 | UP, Ateneo, DLSU, UST, San Beda, Arellano law faculty + grad students |
| Politically engaged citizens (free tier) | 30M | 100K–1M | Event-driven surge during trial; decays after |
| Senate/House staff (impeachment stakeholders) | ~3,000 staff | 200–500 | Senator-judges' staff, prosecution/defense teams |
| NGO/transparency coalitions | ~50 orgs | ~50 seats | #FactsFirstPH, transparency groups |
| Diplomatic missions / foreign press | ~50 embassies | 30–50 seats | Political risk monitoring |

### SAM Sizing (event-on state, e.g., during Jul–Dec 2026 trial)

- **Paid (Pro + Enterprise):** ~7,000–20,000 users × $500 avg blended → **SAM ≈ $3.5M–$10M**
- **Free users:** 100K–1M (no direct revenue; ad/donation/funnel value)
- **Off-cycle SAM** (no active impeachment): collapses ~80% → **SAM ≈ $0.7M–$2M**

**Key insight:** SAM volatility is the central business-model risk. Mitigation = platform extension (see §7).

## 8. Moat & Defensibility Strategy

Ranked by durability (strongest first).

### Tier 1 — Structural Moats (hard to copy)

**1. Data Moat: Ingested PH Impeachment Corpus**
- No LLM has the full text of: 4 Duterte complaints (2024–26), House Justice Committee records, SC ruling *Duterte v. House*, senator-judge statements, historical precedents (Corona, Estrada, Sereno).
- *Defensibility:* High. Requires legal expertise + archival access + months of ingest work. Competitors must repeat this.
- *Action:* Aggressively ingest BEFORE trial; publish a corpus inventory to signal depth.

**2. Domain Moat: PH-Law-Tuned Schema**
- Impeachment-specific ontology: charges (culpable violation, betrayal of trust, graft, bribery, high crimes), procedural stages (complaint → committee → plenary → Senate trial → verdict), SALN fields, confidential-funds categories.
- *Defensibility:* High. Generic LLMs hallucinate PH legal structure; this schema is ground-truth.
- *Action:* Document schema publicly; make it the de facto standard.

### Tier 2 — Network Moats (build over time)

**3. Citation-Graph Network Effect**
- Every newsroom/academic that cites IIP strengthens its authority. More users → more verified citations → more references → more users.
- *Defensibility:* Medium-High (compounds, but slow to start).
- *Action:* Make citation easy (stable URLs, embeddable widgets, CC-BY content).

**4. Brand Moat: "The Canonical Source"**
- GovTrack owns "track Congress." IIP can own "understand impeachment."
- *Defensibility:* Medium. Requires consistent quality + 2–3 years of presence during events.

### Tier 3 — Tactical Moats (buy time, not permanent)

**5. Partnership Exclusivity (anchor media partner)**
- Short-term lock-in (1–2 yr exclusivity with PCIJ/VERA). Buys 12–18 months.
- *Defensibility:* Low-Medium. Partners churn; exclusivity breeds resentment.

**6. Open-Source Moat ("Wikipedia strategy")**
- Open-source the schema + ingest pipeline; keep the hosted corpus + NL layer proprietary.
- *Defensibility:* Paradoxically strong — contributors improve your moat; forks lack the corpus.
- *Action:* Apache 2.0 the toolkit; reference it in academic papers.

### Anti-Moats (avoid these traps)
- ❌ **Pure LLM wrapper** (no defensibility; GPT-5/Claude updates erase any lead)
- ❌ **Paywall-first** (kills citation network effect; civic-tech requires free surface)
- ❌ **Founder-personality dependency** (brand must outlast founders)
