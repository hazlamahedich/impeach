### Key takeaway from the survey
- **No comparable mission-driven civic-tech platform has survived on earned revenue alone.** The closest commercial hybrids (Meedan, Wikimedia Enterprise, Animal Político membership) supplement, not replace, philanthropy.
- The **default civic-tech stack is: anchor foundation grant → Knight-style programmatic grants → individual major donors → reader/membership revenue → API/B2B licensing as a layer 5 ambition.**
- **OpenSecrets ($4.3M expenses, $2.5M revenue, 33% staff cut 2024) and The Markup (sold to CalMatters 2024)** are the cautionary tales: even with marquee funders, pure-play civic intel is financially fragile.

## 3. PH-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

### A. Tax & legal structure for nonprofit
- **SEC registration** as **non-stock, non-profit corporation** (Corporation Code, RA 11232). Requires 5 incorporators, by-laws, stated public-purpose.
- **BIR tax-exempt status**: file for **BIR Tax Exemption Ruling** under Section 30 of NIRC (organized exclusively for scientific, research, or educational purposes; or as a civic league). Must derive no income for stockholders.
- **PCNC (Philippine Council for NGO Certification)** — "Donee Institution" status is the credibility gate for major donor acceptance. PCNC certifies against 8 governance/financial standards; BIR then grants tax-deductibility.
- **Foreign donations** >$10K typically require **BSP / Bangko Sentral reporting** and may require **SEC prior approval** for material foreign grants.
- Consider a **dual structure**: non-stock nonprofit (parent) + a small LLC subsidiary for earned revenue, mirroring Wikimedia/Mozilla/Omidyar Network model. Avoid UBIT (unrelated business income tax) issues by keeping earned activities in the LLC.

### B. Data Privacy Act (DPA) of 2012 (RA 10173) implications for paid users
- If charging customers (B2B SaaS especially), you become a **Personal Information Controller (PIC)** for user identity, billing, and possibly user-submitted content.
- Must register with **NPC (National Privacy Commission)**, appoint a **Data Protection Officer (DPO)**, conduct a **Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA)**, publish a **Privacy Notice** consistent with NPC Circular 2016-02.
- Cross-border transfer of personal data (cloud hosting outside PH) requires NPC-compliant contract clauses.
- For B2B enterprise clients (embassies, law firms), expect ** contractual DPA addenda** referencing NPC + GDPR for international customers.
- **Sensitive personal info** (political opinions, biometrics) requires higher protection tier — careful if you collect political affiliations of users.

### C. Government procurement (slow but stable)
- **RA 9184 (Government Procurement Reform Act)** governs PH gov't purchasing — competitive bidding with thresholds; direct contracting/limited source below P1M goods or P5M consulting.
- Multi-year government contracts are **possible but slow** (Senate Library, UP, DOST-PCASTRD, DICT). Expect 6–18 months from RFP to contract.
- DICT has an explicit mandate for open data / digital gov't — natural anchor partner.
- **Risk**: impeachment is politically charged — any gov't contract could be weaponized. Recommend neutral framing ("governance research infrastructure," "constitutional transparency") and avoid the word "impeachment" in any government-facing pitch.

### D. Newsroom financial distress (B2B media sales headwinds)
- **Rappler SEC revocation saga** (2022 cert of registration revoked, appeals ongoing) — has chilled B2B media spend.
- **ABS-CBN franchise denial (2020)** — collapsed ~11,000 jobs and ~P6B in advertising infrastructure.
- **Pandemic + disinfo economy** — most PH newsrooms operate on skeleton budgets; they cannot be your primary customer. **Free for journalists tier is essential** — you are not selling to them, you are equipping them.
- Realistic B2B customers in PH: **embassies (US/UK/EU/Aus/Canada/Japan)**, **political risk consultancies** (Eurasia Group, Control Risks, FTI), **top 20 law firms** (SyCip, ACCRA, Romulo Maja), **universities (UP, Ateneo, La Salle)**, **international development orgs (World Bank Manila, ADB, UNDP)**.

## 4. REVENUE MODELING (Year 1 / Year 3 / Sustainability)

Based on comparable scale (LittleSis ~$1.5M, MuckRock ~$1M, OpenSecrets $2.5M, DocumentCloud <$1M, The Markup $5M pre-acquisition, PCIJ small-staff ~$1–2M est.):

### Realistic Year-1 budget
- **Total operating budget: $250K–$450K** (P14M–25M)
- **Revenue mix: 90% grants / 5% earned / 5% individual**
- **Headcount: 4–6** (1 founder/ED + 1 senior engineer + 1 data journalist + 1 researcher + 1 part-time ops)
- **Grant sources target**: 2 anchor grants of $100K–$150K each (Omidyar Network + NED or MacArthur), 1 small Knight-style program grant ($30K–$50K), 1 PH CSR sponsorship (P500K–1M from Ayala/Globe)
- **Earned**: 1–2 paid pilot customers at P50K–200K each (~$2K total)
- **Year-1 ARR target: $10K–$30K** (effectively a signal, not a budget line)

### Realistic Year-3 budget
- **Total operating budget: $600K–$1.2M** (P33M–66M)
- **Revenue mix: 55% grants / 30% earned / 15% individual**
- **Headcount: 8–12**
- **Grant sources**: diversified across 3–4 international funders + 2 PH CSR partners + Press Forward-style pooled funds if eligible
- **Earned revenue**: 8–15 paying customers (embassies, law firms, risk consultancies, universities) at $5K–$25K each → **Year-3 ARR target: $150K–$300K**
- **Individual/membership**: 500–1,500 members at P3,500/yr equivalent → $30K–$90K

### Sustainability (Year 5+) — comparable benchmarks
- **LittleSis model**: ~$1.5M annual budget, ~12 staff, primarily foundation-funded with strong individual-donor base and open-source volunteer contributions.
- **MuckRock model**: ~$1M, ~10 staff, freemium SaaS with paid FOIA requests + memberships + Knight grants.
- **DocumentCloud model**: <$1M, ~5 staff, free to journalists, sustained by IRE membership fees + Knight grants.
- **PCIJ model (the closest comp)**: ~13 staff, ~$1–2M est., funded by NED/MacArthur/Open Society + individual major donors, with book sales and fellowships as earned revenue.
- **OpenSecrets model**: $4.3M expenses but $2.5M revenue — **the danger case**. Even marquee funders don't guarantee sustainability.

### Cost-to-serve per user
- **Cloud (GCP/AWS, small-scale, 100–1,000 users)**: $0.50–$3 / active user / mo
- **LLM APIs (Claude/GPT-4 for doc analysis, summarization, search)**: $1–$10 / active user / mo depending on volume; cost falls 50%+/yr
- **Data infrastructure (scraping, scraping, storage, OCR)**: $0.20–$2 / user / mo
- **Customer support, onboarding (B2B)**: $50–$200 / customer / yr
- **Effective cost-to-serve at 500 active users**: ~$3–8K/mo = $36K–$96K/yr — well within a $600K–$1.2M budget

### Case-study synthesis
| Platform | Founded | Now | Lessons |
|---|---|---|---|
| ProPublica (2007) | $10M/yr Sandler anchor | $58M revenue, 209 staff (2024) | **Anchor donor + 17-year compounding** |
| The Markup (2018) | $20M Newmark anchor | Sold to CalMatters (2024) at $5M/yr philanthropy | **Even $20M anchor is not infinite — need diversity by Year 5** |
| LittleSis (2009) | Foundation grants | ~$1.5M, ~12 staff | **Open-source volunteerism is a real labor subsidy** |
| DocumentCloud (2009) | Knight News Challenge | <$1M, ~5 staff, merged into IRE/MuckRock | **Merge-with-bigger-host strategy is viable** |
| MuckRock (2010) | Crowdfunding + Knight | ~$1M, ~10 staff, 501c3 since 2016 | **Freemium with paid microtransactions works at small scale** |
| OpenSecrets (1983) | Carnegie/Hewlett/MacArthur | $2.5M revenue, 1/3 staff cut 2024 | **Funder fatigue is real — diversify aggressively** |
| PCIJ (1989) | NED + MacArthur + OS | ~$1–2M, ~13 staff, 35 years | **Closest PH precedent — long-lived at small scale** |

## 5. RECOMMENDED BUSINESS MODEL + PRICING TIERS

### Recommended structure: **Hybrid Nonprofit Parent + Earned-Revenue Subsidiary**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ IIP Foundation, Inc. (PH non-stock nonprofit)       │
│  • Free public dashboard, API for citizens/journalists│
│  • Grant-funded research, reports, training         │
│  • Owns the data, IP, brand                         │
│  • PCNC-certified, BIR tax-exempt                   │
└─────────────┬───────────────────────────────────────┘
              │ 100% owns
              ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ IIP Labs LLC (or IIP Professional Services)         │
│  • Paid enterprise SaaS, API licensing              │
│  • Custom research for embassies, law firms, risk   │
│  • White-label deployments (regional)               │
│  • Revenues upstream to Foundation                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

This mirrors Mozilla, Wikimedia Enterprise, ProPublica+DocumentCloud, and protects against UBIT while keeping mission alignment.

### Recommended pricing tiers (PH-market)

| Tier | Price (PHP) | Price (USD equiv.) | Target user | Includes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Citizen (Free)** | P0 | $0 | Public, students, citizens | Full public dashboard, search, daily email digest, basic API rate-limited |
| **Journalist/Researcher (Free w/ verification)** | P0 | $0 | Working journalists, academic researchers | Verified badge, higher API limits, embargoed briefings, DocumentCloud-style upload tooling |
| **Pro (Individual)** | **P5,000/yr** | ~$90 | Individual lawyers, consultants, academics | Personal dashboard, alerts, full-text search, export, monthly analyst note |
| **Newsroom / NGO** | **P50,000–P150,000/yr** | ~$900–$2,700 | PH newsrooms, civil society, universities, foreign bureaus | 5–20 seats, shared workspace, API access, priority support, custom data pulls |
| **Enterprise** | **P500,000–P2,000,000/yr** | ~$9,000–$36,000 | Embassies, top 20 law firms, risk consultancies (Control Risks, FTI, Eurasia), ADB/World Bank/UNDP Manila | Unlimited seats, white-glove onboarding, custom research, API SLA, named analyst contact, briefings |
| **API License (commercial)** | Custom ($10K–$100K+/yr) | $10K+ | Embed in third-party products, foreign researchers, regional platforms | Tiered rate limits, dedicated support, data licensing terms |

### Why these prices
- **P5,000/yr Pro** is below Rappler+ (P3,500) effectively competitive with PH willingness-to-pay for individual paid content (~P1,000–5,000 sweet spot).
- **P50K–150K Newsroom/NGO** matches PCIJ/Rappler-sized newsroom budget capacity for a single tool (they currently pay similar for Meltwater/Cision equivalents).
- **P500K–2M Enterprise** is the PH market equivalent of FiscalNote entry-tier in USD, calibrated for embassy/risk-consultancy budgets that benchmark against global contracts.

## TL;DR — Who actually pays for civic tech?

The honest answer from the comparables:

1. **Foundations pay for the mission** (80–95% of revenue for the first 3–5 years). Omidyar Network, NED, Knight, OSF, Ford, MacArthur, Hewlett, ICFJ, GNI are the realistic pool. **Omidyar is the single highest-leverage target for a PH product.**
2. **Enterprise customers pay for the product** (5–20% of revenue in Years 1–3, growing to 30%+ by Year 5). Embassies, top law firms, risk consultancies, universities — **realistic Y1 capture 8–12 customers totaling $30K–$80K**.
3. **Individuals rarely pay enough to matter** in PH (Rappler+ standard P3,500/yr; even at 1,000 members = only $60K/yr). But they build political legitimacy and create a defense against "foreign-funded" attacks.
4. **Government contracts are slow, prestigious, and politically risky** — pursue only post-Year-3 and only with neutral framing.
5. **Hybrid nonprofit+LLC structure** (Mozilla/Wikimedia Enterprise model) is the right container — protects mission, enables enterprise revenue, qualifies for PCNC/BIR tax exemption.

**Year-1 target**: **$300K total operating budget**, 90% grants, 4–6 headcount, free for citizens/journalists, 2–4 paid enterprise pilots.

**Year-3 target**: **$800K–$1.2M total budget**, 55% grants / 30% earned / 15% individual, 8–12 headcount, $200K+ ARR.

**Year-5 sustainability benchmark**: PCIJ-scale (~$1–2M, ~13 staff, 35-year track record) is achievable; The Markup-scale ($5M+) requires either a Newmark-style anchor or absorption into a larger institution.
