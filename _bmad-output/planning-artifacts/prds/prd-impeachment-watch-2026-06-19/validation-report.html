Fair

The PRD is structurally strong — all seven rubric dimensions land at strong or adequate, with no thin or broken dimensions. The thesis (citation-or-silence) is genuinely load-bearing, the Finalize cycle (D-014…D-028) resolved the hard governance/legal/omission problems, and the document is decision-ready and downstream-usable.

The risk is not structural; it is definitional. Two binding requirements use terms that are never operationally defined — "served assertion" (EI-1) and "independent sources" (EI-2) — which makes the headline 100% citation-coverage and fact-vs-claim claims enforcement-soft rather than mechanically enforced. The adversarial and editorial-integrity reviewers independently surfaced the same pattern: the integrity invariants are strong on coverage (everything has a tag, a citation, a substring check) and weak on correctness (is the tag right? is the citation the right one? is the gate manned?).

Additionally, the Pre-External Presentation Gate — the load-bearing governance control for external presentation — is mandatory in form but has no assigned counsel (OQ-4), making it a phantom gate today. None of this is fatal; all of it is fixable without restructuring the PRD. But the PRD currently presents these as resolved when they are open.

Decision-readiness

strong

Decisions are stated as decisions, not buried as considerations. Trade-offs name what was given up: conservative merge accepts "a duplicate node is a cosmetic defect; an incorrect merge corrupts every downstream answer" (§6.1 EI-5); local-first is "a binding v1 constraint, not a preference" (§9.7 NFR-D-1); governance is "engineering-enforced day-to-day + a mandatory Pre-External Presentation Gate" rather than either pole alone (§6.2, D-014). Strategic coherence

strong

The thesis is stated, repeated, and prioritized: "the integrity core comes first and the flashier capabilities (narrative generation, contradiction engine) are deliberately deferred until that core is proven" (§2). Feature scope in §5.1 follows from the thesis — ingestion, extraction, Q&A with citations, graph/timeline/evidence views, editorial invariants — while §5.2 defers narrative, media comparison, contradiction engine, influence analytics, streaming, and SaaS. This is not a backlog with section headings; the prioritization logic is visible.

Success metrics validate the thesis rather than measuring activity: SM-1 (citation 100%), SM-2 (allegation-as-fact 0), SM-3 (merge-error ≈0), SM-4b (refutes recall ≥70%), SM-7 (adversarial demo 100%). Counter-metrics are named — CM-1 against SM-1 ("over-silence is a failure mode"), CM-2 against SM-5 ("latency is never bought by dropping the citation gate"). The MVP scope kind is coherent: problem-solving + experience.

The one soft spot: SM-4 ("Extraction accuracy >85%") and SM-4a ("Query answer accuracy >90%") are marked "per north-star PRD" — inherited targets, not earned here. No eval methodology is referenced for v1 beyond NFR-O-2's CI gates, and the thresholds read as round-number inheritance.

Adversarial review

review-adversarial-general.md

A PRD that is unusually disciplined on coverage but quietly soft on correctness — the hardest invariants are either self-graded, circular, or tests of a weaker property than they advertise, and the mandatory legal gate has no one to man it.

critical

Refutes-edge recall is circular/self-graded

§9.1 NFR-EI-5, SM-4b
The team builds the eval fixture that defines the denominator, so ≥70% measures "did we find what we already knew was there," not "did we find what we missed." A self-authored ground truth cannot detect evidence the team didn't think to look for.
Fix: The refutes-edge eval fixture must be authored or double-annotated by someone independent who reads the source corpus to determine ground truth — not the extractor's builder.
critical

Allegation-as-fact gate measures coverage, not correctness

§9.1 NFR-EI-7, SM-2, SM-7
NFR-EI-7 enforces that a tag exists, not the right tag. The defamation-grade P0 only fires on curated questions the team already wrote. Coverage ≠ correctness — the PRD conflates the two.
Fix: Separate tag-coverage (100%, mechanical) from tag-correctness (sampled audit on non-curated live queries, with a floor and owner).
critical

Mandatory legal gate is a phantom

§13.1 OQ-4, FR-5.5, NFR-L-3
The Pre-External Gate is "mandatory" but counsel is an unassigned open item. A mandatory gate with no assigned reviewer cannot fire. Today the gate is an intention, not a control.
Fix: Make retained cyberlibel counsel a pre-build gate, or name a fallback (defer presentation until counsel is retained). Treat "assignment open" as a P0 scheduling risk.
critical

Fact/claim tag correctness depends on extraction; gate only checks coverage

§9.1 NFR-EI-7, FR-2.5
The fact/claim boundary rule is presented as deterministic but is a classifier output gated by a presence check. The hard gate verifies a tag exists, not that it's right. This is the single most dangerous conflation in the document.
Fix: Rename NFR-EI-7 to "fact/claim tag coverage 100%." Add a separate, sampled correctness metric audited on non-curated queries.
high

"≥2 independent sources" is undefined and a hard research problem

§6.1 EI-2
Two outlets citing the same Senate press release are not independent. Wire-syndicated stories (Reuters → PNA republish) are the same source twice. Determining independence requires source-derivation analysis the extractor is not specified to perform.
Fix: Define "independent" operationally; require the extractor to record the independence basis; default to "attributed" when independence can't be established.
high

No rule for tier-1-vs-tier-1 source conflict

§6.1 EI-2, §6.3
Under the current rule, each side independently satisfies "fact = tier-1," so two contradictory facts can both be served as established. House vs Senate framing of the Duterte impeachment is core contested material.
Fix: Add an explicit rule: when ≥2 tier-1 sources conflict on the same assertion, neither is served as "fact" — both are attributed claims with the conflict visible.
high

Local-model quality thresholds asserted without evidence; NFR-D-2 may be fiction

§9.7 NFR-D-1/D-2, §9.8 NFR-O-2
No cited evidence that Qwen2.5-14B or Llama-3.1-8B can hit groundedness ≥0.95 or answer accuracy >90% on Philippine political/legal English. If only the cloud tier clears the hard gate, "cloud never required" is a polite fiction.
Fix: Run a pre-build feasibility check on a pilot corpus slice; if local models can't hit the gates, either lower the gates or acknowledge the cloud tier is required for some tasks.
high

Substring gate over-presented as "anti-hallucination backstop"

§6.1 EI-6, FR-3.2
Substring validation catches fabricated quotes. It does not catch misattribution, wrong-document lift, or context-stripping ("alleged X" → "X"). A reader would reasonably conclude "100% quote-validity" means "no hallucinated content reaches the user," which is false.
Fix: Rename EI-6 to "quote-existence validation"; name what it does not catch; add uncaught failure modes to RK.
high

DPA compliance review deferred but external presentation authorized

§9.4 NFR-L-2, FR-5.5
The DPA review is deferred to "beyond the internal period," but external presentation to journalists is authorized within v1. The boundary is undefined; the cyberlibel review does not cover data-privacy posture for presenting aggregated personal data to third parties.
Fix: Fold a DPA posture review into the Pre-External Gate, or explicitly classify external presentation as "internal" for DPA purposes and say why.
medium

SM-8's "presentation landed" signal rigged toward easy disjunct

§10.3 SM-8
"A concrete follow-up request" from a single audience member is a very low bar OR'd with the harder ≥50% spot-verification. The first presentation is graded against placeholder thresholds — the bar is set after the jump.
Fix: Make conjunctive for first presentation (both interest and verification), or name the easy disjunct as a minimum-viable-interest signal.

"Independent reviewers" independence is aspirational, not structural

§9.8 NFR-O-2, SM-7, D-024
"Where feasible" and "at least in part" are hedges. Nothing structurally prevents reviewers from being adjacent to the build team (same org, same funding). "Rotating" addresses bias drift, not capture.
Fix: Define "independent"; make at least one reviewer structurally external (contracted, not volunteered); drop "where feasible."
medium

Retraction hook is a stub presented as a v1 feature

FR-5.7, NFR-L-5, DR-6, OQ-10
The platform flags, but what it does with a flagged answer is undefined. The legal review that defines behavior hasn't been commissioned. The demo will show something, but that something is not yet designed.
Fix: Define v1 retraction behavior fully before demoing it, or remove DR-6 from v1 demo readiness.
medium

Determinism gate tests reproducibility, not correctness

§9.8 NFR-O-2, FR-2.4
Projection-determinism verifies same inputs → same graph, not that the graph is correct. Reproducibly incorrect passes the gate. A reader could conclude "deterministic = trustworthy," which is false.
Fix: Label as rebuildability/reproducibility gate; pair with a correctness metric on the graph (edge precision/recall on a sampled subgraph).

Corpus targets are "indicative" with no floor

No floor below which the demo is cancelled. "Operator confirms actuals" means the targets are vibes with numbers next to them.

If Ollama hangs or OOMs on stage, the entire pitch collapses with no fallback. A live demo that dies on stage is worse than no demo.

Editorial integrity review

review-editorial-integrity.md

The editorial-integrity architecture is genuinely strong — citation-or-silence as a code-path absence, the Pre-External Gate, the honest evidence split, and the cyberlibel acknowledgment are better than most products in this space attempt. But two critical definitional gaps — "served assertion" and "independent sources" — make the headline 100% claims enforcement-soft; the substring gate is a quote-integrity check, not a claim-accuracy check; and several binding requirements use undefined terms or structurally unfalsifiable metrics.

critical

"Served assertion" boundary is undefined

§6.1 EI-1, §9.1 NFR-EI-1, §10 SM-1
100% citation coverage is answer-level, not assertion-level. Uncited clauses can leak through LLM framing prose. An LLM-generated answer's synthesizing/connective language about a named public figure is the exact failure mode the product exists to prevent.
Fix: Define "served assertion" as every declarative clause making a factual/attributed claim; enforce per-clause citation via a post-generation parser; add a CI test for uncited declarative clauses in generated answers.
critical

"Independent sources" is undefined — wire-story propagation makes fact threshold gameable

§6.1 EI-2
In Philippine media, wire stories propagate across multiple outlets. Without a provenance-de-duplication rule, a single-origin wire allegation republished by multiple outlets gets tagged as established fact — defamation-grade and invisible to the substring gate.
Fix: Define independence operationally (distinct original reporting, not republishing the same wire/agency feed); track upstream feed provenance per source; de-duplicate by origin, not outlet count.
high

Substring validation catches fabricated quotes, not fabricated or mischaracterized claims

§6.1 EI-6, §8 FR-3.2
Does not catch: mischaracterization (real quote, wrong framing), unquoted fabrication (paraphrased claim with real citation that doesn't support it), or misattribution (real quote, wrong speaker). The gate is a quote-integrity gate, not a claim-accuracy gate.
Fix: Acknowledge EI-6's scope; name uncovered modes; add a misattribution check and a claim-to-passage consistency check to the eval harness.
high

"Corroboration signal" is undefined in a binding requirement

§6.1 EI-8, §8 FR-5.6, §9.1 NFR-EI-8
"Corroboration signal" is not defined. What counts? A second tier-3 source? A tier-2 mentioning the same allegation? Without a definition, the citation-quality floor is a guideline, not a gate.
Fix: Define corroboration operationally; add an eval case for a lone tier-3 defamatory allegation with no corroboration.
high

Refutes-recall denominator is eval-fixture-dependent and potentially unrepresentative

§9.1 NFR-EI-5, §10 SM-4b
The denominator is only knowable for the curated fixture, not the live corpus. No minimum fixture size, source-type diversity, or adversarial-case composition is specified. A 70% on an easy fixture is worse than honest silence.
Fix: Specify minimum fixture requirements; report fixture composition alongside the metric; add a stretch target on the adversarial subset.
medium

Merge-error rate is structurally unfalsifiable as stated

§9.1 NFR-EI-3, §10 SM-3
Conservative merging produces few merge errors by construction — you don't make the merges you're unsure about. The ≈0 target is nearly tautological. The real risk (unbounded duplicate rate) is "accepted as the tradeoff" but not bounded.
Fix: Measure merge precision/recall on a held-out labeled set; bound the duplicate rate rather than accepting it unboundedly.
medium

Retraction interim behavior underspecified

FR-5.7, NFR-L-5, OQ-10
Under Philippine cyberlibel, continued surfacing of a retracted allegation (even with a marker) may be republication. The hook detects the problem; the response should be conservative by default.
Fix: Default to suppression of retracted defamatory content until the legal-review-defined workflow is in place; the conservative default is suppression, not downgrade.
medium

"Where feasible" weakens external independence for the adversarial set

SM-4a, SM-7, RK-14
"Where feasible" / "at least in part" are soft qualifiers. If external authorship is infeasible (inconvenient), the adversarial set is self-authored — the exact self-grading bias RK-14 names.
Fix: Make external authorship a requirement with a defined fallback gate; specify minimum external portion (e.g., ≥30%).
low

Filipino-language model-quality risk understated; no fallback defined

§13.2 OQ-9
Local models are not Filipino-specialized. The gate is rigorous in form but may pass without proving real-world extraction quality. If fil fails the gate, the product is English-only — a material coverage gap for a Philippine corpus.
Fix: Acknowledge model-quality risk; define fallback (English-only with stated coverage gap disclosed in demo per DR-4).
