# Spine Pair Review — Impeachment Watch

## Overall verdict
This is a **strong** spine pair — a regulated-leaning product where every load-bearing decision is committed and traceable. The citation-or-silence / fact-vs-claim / source-verb / trust-tier / no-oversell / supersession integrity rules are carried identically through decision log → DESIGN tokens → EXPERIENCE behavioral specs → Key Flow climaxes, which is what a downstream consumer (architecture, story-dev) needs to source-extract without ambiguity. Two mechanical defects hold it back from a clean "strong across the board": (1) `spacing.20` and `spacing.24` are referenced in component specs but never defined in the `spacing:` frontmatter (only the three named overrides are); (2) the `sources:` list in EXPERIENCE.md points at three research directories that don't exist at the stated paths (`domain-research/`, `market-research/`, `technical-research/` — actual files live under `planning-artifacts/research/`). Everything else resolves cleanly.

## 1. Flow coverage — strong

What was checked: every named user journey / requirement in EXPERIENCE.md Key Flows has a named protagonist, numbered steps, a marked climax beat, and at least one failure path.

### Findings
- **Flow inventory.** Two flows: (1) Maya Reyes, investigative journalist — the case spine, cited from PRD §7.2 and decision log Journey 1; (2) Renz Aquino, political-risk analyst — the actor spine, cited from decision log Journey 2 (accepted). Both follow the canonical 8-step shape from the Drift example and the decision log.
- **Protagonist naming.** Both flows name the protagonist in the heading and step 1. Decision-log names match verbatim (`Maya Reyes`, `Renz Aquino`).
- **Climax beats.** Flow 1 climax = citation-or-silence refusal ("No sourced answer found"); Flow 2 climax = no-prediction honesty ("IIP does not make predictions..."). Both climaxes are explicitly marked `**Climax:**` and re-state the exact verbatim copy from the decision log and Voice & Tone table — verbatim consistency is a load-bearing commitment for the regulated-leaning posture, and it holds.
- **Failure paths.** Flow 1 carries three explicit failure paths (query timeout, backing service degraded fail-closed, document unavailable). Flow 2 carries three (thin senator dashboard = honest absence, predictive question = no-prediction first-class state, graph hop-cap = non-blocking banner). Failure paths are behavioral, not decorative.
- **Surfaces exercised.** Both flows list the surfaces exercised at the foot, and every v1 consumer surface appears in at least one flow: `/chat`, `/claim/[id]`, `/graph`, `/timeline`, `/evidence/compare`, `/senators/[id]`, `/documents/[id]`, `<Citation>`. This matches the discovery-closure surface matrix in the decision log verbatim. No orphan surfaces; no unsurfaced requirement.
- **high** (EXPERIENCE.md:35–47): Operator surfaces (Source registry, ingestion dashboard, extraction-quality spot-check, dead-letter triage, Pre-External Gate review, retraction/correction display) are declared out of external-presentation scope and have no Key Flow. *Fix:* this is explicitly defensible (single-sentence scope statement), but a one-line note in Key Flows acknowledging the deferral would close the consumer's "did they forget?" loop.

## 2. Token completeness — adequate

Contrast targets stated for load-bearing combinations.

### Findings
- **Color tokens.** All 21 light-mode and 21 dark-mode color tokens have hex values in the frontmatter. Light/dark pairs are present for every load-bearing token (`primary`, `accent`, `surface-*`, `border`, `muted`, `muted-foreground`, all three `trust-tier-*`, all three `claim-*`, `defamation-risk-caution`, `destructive`). The shadcn-inherited tokens (`background`, `foreground`, `popover`, `card`, `input`, `ring`, `secondary`) are explicitly listed as inherited in the comment header at DESIGN.md:5–9 — consistent with the editorial example pattern.
- **Typography tokens.** `display`, `display-sm`, `headline`, `body-lg`, `body-md`, `label-caps`, `caption`, `mono`, `mono-sm` — all defined with fontFamily / fontSize / fontWeight / lineHeight (and letterSpacing where it matters). `body-md` uses the `note: 'shadcn default — inherited'` form sanctioned by the spec (DESIGN.md:107, spec line 48) — correct.
- **Rounded tokens.** `sm` 3px, `md` 5px, `lg` 8px, `xl` 12px, `full` 9999px — all defined.
- **Token reference resolution.** Every `{colors.*}`, `{typography.*}`, `{rounded.*}` reference in DESIGN.md prose resolves to a defined frontmatter token (26 unique refs checked). EXPERIENCE.md references resolve cleanly too — every `{colors.accent}`, `{colors.border}`, `{colors.claim-dashed}`, `{colors.surface-sunken}`, `{colors.trust-tier-*}`, `{colors.primary}`, `{colors.defamation-risk-caution}` is defined in DESIGN.md.
- **critical** (DESIGN.md:145–149 vs 308, 322, 436, 437): `{spacing.20}` and `{spacing.24}` are referenced in `senator-card.padding` and `answer-block.padding` and re-stated in the prose Component specs, but the `spacing:` frontmatter block (DESIGN.md:144–149) defines only `editorial-gap`, `graph-panel`, `evidence-split-gap`. The comment claims "shadcn / Tailwind 4-based scale inherited (4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 32, 40, 48, 64)" — but inherited numeric tokens are not actually emitted as YAML keys, so a source-extractor resolving `{spacing.20}` literally will fail. *Fix:* either emit `20: '20px'` and `24: '24px'` in the `spacing:` block, or change the component references to literal `20px` / `24px`. The inherited-scale convention is fine in prose; it is not fine inside `{...}` resolver syntax. This is the one finding that will break a mechanical source-extractor.
- **high** (DESIGN.md, Colors section): contrast targets are stated in prose ("verified to AA 4.5:1 (text) and 3:1 (UI components / graphical objects)" at EXPERIENCE.md:156) and called out load-bearing in DESIGN.md:386 ("The semantic tokens shift value (not hue) between modes to maintain AA contrast"). However, the DESIGN.md Colors section itself does not restate the AA targets per token — it relies on EXPERIENCE.md → Accessibility Floor to carry the contrast contract. This is a defensible split (contrast is behavioral/verification, not a token value), but a one-line "Contrast: AA 4.5:1 against `surface-base` / `surface-raised`; verified for both modes" anchor in DESIGN.md Colors would make DESIGN.md standalone-readable for an architecture consumer who never opens EXPERIENCE.md. *Fix:* add a two-sentence contrast statement to the Colors section header.

## 6. Bloat & overspecification — strong

What was checked: pixel specs where tokens cover it; source restatement (personas, FRs, scope); prose where a table works; sections no downstream consumer would read; decorative narrative untied to a decision. DESIGN.md prose may carry editorial voice; EXPERIENCE.md prose should not.

- **DESIGN.md editorial voice.** The Brand & Style and Colors sections carry a strong editorial voice ("the way a legal journal sets a case header", "warm parchment white in light mode, modern dark in dark mode", "the silence is the answer"). This is sanctioned by the spec (the editorial example is the analog) and every editorial turn is tied to a load-bearing decision (the serif is tied to "this is the record", the warm-white base is tied to "court-document credibility", the dark toggle is tied to "interactive surface at night"). - **EXPERIENCE.md prose discipline.** EXPERIENCE.md is table-heavy and rule-dense. The Foundation and Information Architecture sections carry necessary technical contract detail (RSC fetch, React Query 5, Zustand stores, nuqs URL keys, `lib/state/url-keys.ts`). - **Source restatement.** The Voice & Tone table restates verbatim copy that also appears in PRD §6.3 and the decision log. This is intentional — the spine is the binding contract, and the verbatim copy must live in the spine for the story-dev consumer to cite without opening the PRD. - **No pixel specs where tokens cover.** Component specs use token references (`{rounded.md}`, `{spacing.24}`, `{colors.surface-sunken}`) rather than restating pixel values. The one exception is `padding: '2px 10px'` on the citation chip (DESIGN.md:158) — a compound value not covered by a single token, acceptable.
- **low** (EXPERIENCE.md:235–253 Editorial Integrity Surface): this section re-states rules already present in Voice & Tone, Component Patterns, and State Patterns, organized under the EI-1 through EI-8 / FR-5.x / NFR-L-3 codes. Keep.
- **low** (EXPERIENCE.md:255–276 Narrative Beats): 15-row table mapping domain beats to UX surfaces. Could be argued as scope creep (narrative generation is deferred to Phase 2). But the table maps beats to *timeline markers and graph highlights* — which are v1 surfaces — not to generated prose. The "Rules" footer explicitly says "Beats surface as markers and highlights, not generated narrative." Earns its place.
