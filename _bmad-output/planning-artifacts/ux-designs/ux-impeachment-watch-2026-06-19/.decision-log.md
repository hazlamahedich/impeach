## Named-protagonist journeys

### Journey 1 — Maya Reyes, investigative journalist (the case spine; from PRD §7.2)

Top-down: enters through the case, traces claims and evidence.

1. Maya lands on `/chat`, types: *"What allegations have been made about the confidential funds, and who made them?"*
2. Platform returns a sourced answer — each assertion carries inline citation chips. She clicks one.
3. Citation modal opens: verbatim quoted passage, trust tier visible, source-verb preserved. She clicks "View full document."
4. Lands on `/documents/[id]`, scrolled to exact passage. Reads surrounding context.
5. Goes to `/evidence/compare` — sees honest split: supporting vs. refuting vs. contextualizing. It's one-sided; platform says so plainly: *"Only supporting evidence detected in v1."*
6. Opens `/graph?seed=respondent` — graph opens centered on Sara Duterte (Trace default), immediate neighbors. Clicks through to a senator who voted against her.
7. Checks `/timeline` — scrubs to filing date, watches procedural stages unfold.
8. **Climax:** She asks an adversarial question the corpus can't support. Platform responds: **"No sourced answer found."** Her trust goes *up* — it refused to invent. She cites the platform in her story.

Surfaces exercised: /chat, <Citation> modal, /documents/[id], /evidence/compare, /graph, /timeline. Trust signal: citation-or-silence refusal.

### Journey 2 — Renz Aquino, political-risk analyst (the actor spine; UX-drafted)

Bottom-up: enters through a single actor, threads the case from their perspective.

> Renz Aquino, analyst at a Manila-based political-risk consultancy (Bower Group Asia type). Tuesday afternoon. A corporate client wants a briefing on likely Senate vote trajectories — specifically which senator-judges lean which way, and what their track records signal.

1. Renz lands on `/senators/[id]` for Senator Risa Hontiveros. Lightweight dashboard: statements on record, past votes, committee participation, personal timeline entries tied to the case.
2. He sees a claim: "Senator Hontiveros stated X on [date]." Tagged as attributed claim (not fact), with citation chip. He clicks it.
3. Lands on `/claim/[id]` — addressable claim surface. Sees full claim, provenance, source document. Copies the URL, sends to a colleague: *"here's the exact claim, sourced."*
4. Opens `/graph?seed=senator-hontiveros` — graph opens centered on her (Trace default): committee memberships, votes, statements, co-sponsors. Switches to Temporal mode, scrubs the timeline — watches her statements cluster around key hearing dates.
5. Queries in `/chat`: *"What statements has Senator Hontiveros made about the confidential funds issue?"* Gets sourced answer with citation chips.
6. Pivots to another senator-judge — publicly undecided. Opens `/senators/[id]`. Dashboard is thinner: fewer statements, no past impeachment votes. Platform shows what it has and is honest about what it doesn't.
7. **Climax:** He asks: *"Will Senator X vote to convict?"* — a predictive question. Platform responds: **"IIP does not make predictions. Here is what is on record: [sourced statements, voting history, relationships]. Draw your own inference."** Renz respects this — the platform gave him raw material for analysis without overstepping. He writes his briefing citing the platform's sourced data, noting explicitly that it refused to predict.

Surfaces exercised: /senators/[id], /claim/[id], /graph (Trace + Temporal), /chat. Trust signal: no-prediction / no-oversell honesty.

(driver: UX drafted — user to confirm or revise)
